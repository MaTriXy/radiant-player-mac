Release notes for Radiant Player v1.4.0:

At long last, the actual next version!

Major changes:

  • Support Google Play Music's latest redesign
  • Mac OS X 10.8 is no longer supported (see #291 for progress, if any, on the possibility of re-instating support)
  • Removed all styles except for Google and Black (formerly Spotify Black) due to incompatibility with the new redesign; I will be looking into porting one or two of the original styles to the new design

Other changes:

  • Prevent brief 'ghost' mini player while switching spaces (fixes #309)
  • Allow mini player to be used in other full screen applications (fixes #79)
  • Allow reload button to be clicked when internet connectivity is lost while first loading Google Play Music (fixes #313)
  • Implement ⌘A (select all) shortcut (fixes #317)
  • Open release page instead of home page when a new version is available
  • Prevent dragging of file onto main window (fixes #307, thanks @nielstholenaar)
  • Add support for ⌘[ and ⌘] keyboard shortcuts to go forward and backward (fixes #296, thanks @gholts)

Many thanks to all the testers of the two beta releases, and I will certainly be continuing to look into the outstanding issues that a couple users have reported. Also, very many thanks to everybody who was patiently waiting for this release ever since the Google Play Music redesign! I would have liked a much shorter release window, but hopefully this version is satisfactory enough to forgive that! Enjoy.
Media keys

Control your music without having to switch applications. Radiant Player responds to the media keys built into your Mac, allowing you to easily control the playback of your music no matter what you're doing.

Notifications

Get notifications whenever the currently playing song changes.

Mini player

Use the mini player in the system menu bar to control your music without interrupting you.
Alternate between mini player sizes by clicking on the album art.

Last.fm Integration

Update your Now Playing status and scrobble tracks to Last.fm.

Adjustable appearance

Use custom styles built into Radiant Player to make it look beautiful and integrated into your system, or stick with Google's original theme.
